ANNEXURE B – DATA PROTECTION

1.​ Definitions

For the purpose of this Annexure: 1.1​ "Data Protection Laws" means all applicable data protection and privacy laws in force from time to time in the UK, the EU, the US, and South Africa, including but not limited to the UK Data Protection Act 2018, the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR), relevant US federal or state privacy laws, and the South African Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA). 1.2​ "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. 1.3​ "Processing" means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

2.​ Compliance with Data Protection Laws

Both parties shall comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws. This includes, but is not limited to, ensuring that all Personal Data is processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently, and that appropriate technical and organizational measures are in place to protect the Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

3.​ Data Controller and Data Processor

3.1.​ Where one party is the Data Controller and the other party is the Data Processor, the Data Processor shall: 3.1.1.​ Process the Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Data Controller. 3.1.2.​ Ensure that persons authorized to process the Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality. 3.1.3.​ Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk. 3.1.4.​ Not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Data Controller. 3.1.5.​ Assist the Data Controller in ensuring compliance with the Data Controller’s obligations pursuant to Data Protection Laws, including but not limited to data subject rights requests and data protection impact assessments.

3.1.6.​ At the choice of the Data Controller, delete or return all Personal Data to the Data Controller after the end of the provision of services relating to processing, and delete existing copies unless applicable law requires storage of the Personal Data. 3.1.7.​ Make available to the Data Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in this clause and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Data Controller or another auditor mandated by the Data Controller.

4.​ International Data Transfers

4.1​ Any transfer of Personal Data from the UK, the EU, the US, or South Africa to a country outside these regions shall be conducted in compliance with the Data Protection Laws. 4.2​ Transfers of Personal Data from the EU or the UK to a third country or an international organization shall be based on an adequacy decision by the European Commission or the UK Information Commissioner’s Office, or subject to appropriate safeguards as provided by the GDPR or UK Data Protection Act 2018. 4.3​ Transfers of Personal Data within the US must comply with applicable federal and state privacy laws, ensuring the protection of data subject rights. 4.4​ Transfers of Personal Data from South Africa to a third country or international organization shall comply with the conditions for cross-border transfers set out in POPIA.

5.​ Data Subject Rights

Both parties shall assist each other in responding to any request from a data subject and in ensuring compliance with their obligations under Data Protection Laws with respect to security, breach notifications, impact assessments, and consultations with supervisory authorities or regulators.

6.​ Data Breach Notification

In the event of a Personal Data breach, the party responsible for the breach shall notify the other party without undue delay and shall cooperate with the other party to comply with its obligations under Data Protection Laws.

7.​ Indemnity

Each party shall indemnify the other against any claims, actions, liabilities, losses, damages, and expenses incurred by the other party arising from any breach of the Data Protection Laws by the indemnifying party, its employees, or agents.
